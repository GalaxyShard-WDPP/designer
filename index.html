Robert Jaeger

Robert Jaeger is a game developer and designer from Marlboro, New Jersey. In his teenage years he created many games for consoles like the Atari. Inspired by the games of his time like Pac-Man and Donkey Kong, he dedicated himself to learning programming so that he could publish his own projects and bring his ideas to life.
The most successful and unique project he made was a platformer called Montezuma's Revenge. It was ported over to several systems, like the Atari 2600, Atari 5200, and the Sega Master System.
Prior to developing Montezuma's Revenge, his primary projects were remasters of existing games. For example, his game Pinhead was based on the popular game of it's time Kickman
During the height of Robert Jaeger's career, computers were not powerful enough to process intense graphics. Despite this, his games makes up for this through carefully designed and architected code that could take computers to their limit. His game Montezuma's Revenge, for example, consists of countless different mechanics all of which fit together in a way that makes Robert's style incomparable to the current state of game design
